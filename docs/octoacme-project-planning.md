## Purpose
Turn an approved initiative into an actionable plan and backlog for delivery.

## Objectives
- Break work into shippable increments
- Identify dependencies and risks
- Align timelines, releases, and responsibilities

## Activities
1. Kickoff meeting with stakeholders and delivery team
2. Create prioritized backlog with acceptance criteria (led by Product Manager and Business Analyst)
3. Estimate scope (T-shirt sizing or story points)
4. Define Definition of Done (DoD) with input from QA Lead
5. Identify dependencies and integration points
6. Create release plan and milestone map (coordinated with Release Manager)
7. Establish agile ceremonies and team rhythm (facilitated by Scrum Master)
8. Define UX/design deliverables and review cycles (if UX/UI Designer is involved)

## Sprint / Iteration Planning
- Timebox planning to agreed sprint length
- Pull items that meet DoD and have clear acceptance criteria
- Ensure team capacity is respected

## Risk & Dependency Management
- Capture in Risk Register:
  - ID, Description, Impact, Probability, Owner, Mitigation
- Mark cross-team dependencies in the project board and escalate during weekly syncs

## Planning Checklist
- [ ] Project kickoff held with all key roles identified
- [ ] Backlog prioritized and estimated (Product Manager, Business Analyst)
- [ ] Release timeline and milestones agreed (Release Manager, Project Manager)
- [ ] Definition of Done documented (QA Lead, team consensus)
- [ ] Initial test plan / QA approach drafted (QA Lead)
- [ ] Design deliverables and review cycles defined (UX/UI Designer, if applicable)
- [ ] Sprint/iteration cadence and ceremonies scheduled (Scrum Master)
- [ ] Role responsibilities and handoff points clarified across team
